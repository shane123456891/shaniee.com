The Persistence of Memory

The Persistence of Memory is a surrealistic masterpiece painted by the Spanish artist Salvador Dalí in 1931. The painting is known for its melting clocks draped over various objects, symbolizing the fluidity and relativity of time. This piece is one of Dalí's most iconic works and exemplifies his unique style of dream-like imagery. The painting is currently housed in the Museum of Modern Art (MoMA) in New York.
