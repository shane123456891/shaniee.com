Girl with a Pearl Earring

"Girl with a Pearl Earring" is a famous painting by Dutch artist Johannes Vermeer, created around 1665. Often referred to as the "Mona Lisa of the North," the painting is a tronie, which is a study of a character or type rather than a portrait of a specific person. The work is known for its striking use of light and shadow, the mysterious expression of the girl, and of course, the large pearl earring she wears. It is housed at the Mauritshuis museum in The Hague, Netherlands.
