The Scream

"The Scream" is one of the most iconic paintings by Norwegian artist Edvard Munch, created in 1893. It is a symbol of existential angst and despair, depicting a figure standing on a bridge with a distorted, screaming face. The work is famous for its swirling colors and emotional intensity, capturing a moment of intense psychological anguish. It is often seen as a representation of modern life's anxieties. Several versions of *The Scream* exist, including paintings, lithographs, and pastels. The painting is housed in the National Gallery in Oslo, Norway.
